# YC Pitch Deck Template

## Slide 1: Company Name + One-Liner

**[Company Name]**
is [what you do] for [who you serve]
that [key benefit/differentiator].

Example: "Stripe is payments infrastructure for internet businesses that makes accepting money as easy as embedding a few lines of code."

---

## Slide 2: Problem

**[Specific problem your users face]**

- Describe one concrete pain point
- How do users solve this today? (Badly, manually, expensively?)
- Quantify the pain if possible (dollars wasted, hours lost)

Example: "Small businesses spend 15+ hours/week on manual invoicing, and 30% of invoices are paid late. Existing solutions cost $200+/month and are built for enterprises, not them."

---

## Slide 3: Solution

**[How you solve the problem]**

- What your product does in 1-2 sentences
- Screenshot or demo GIF (preferred)
- Why this is 10x better than alternatives

Example: "InvoiceJet auto-generates invoices from timesheets, sends automated reminders, and processes payments—all in one click. Setup takes 5 minutes, not 5 days."

---

## Slide 4: Traction

**[Numbers that prove this works]**

- Revenue: $[X] MRR | [X]% MoM growth
- Users: [X] active | [X]% WoW growth
- Engagement: [X]% D7 retention | [X] actions/user/day
- Other: [X] waitlist | [X] NPS | [X]% "very disappointed" in PMF survey

Pick 2-3 strongest metrics. Lead with your best number.

---

## Slide 5: Market

**[Why this is a big opportunity]**

- TAM: [Total Addressable Market] ($[X]B)
- SAM: [Serviceable Addressable Market] ($[X]B)
- SOM: [Serviceable Obtainable Market] ($[X]M — what you can realistically capture)
- Market trends working in your favor

OR: "If we're right that [insight about the market], the market is at least $[X]B."

---

## Slide 6: Product

**[How it works — briefly]**

- 3-5 steps max showing the user journey
- Screenshot/visual walkthrough
- Highlight the "aha moment"

---

## Slide 7: Business Model

**[How you make money]**

- Pricing: $[X]/month per seat | $[X] per transaction | [X]% take rate
- Unit economics: LTV $[X] | CAC $[X] | LTV:CAC [X]:1
- Payback period: [X] months

If early, show expected pricing and preliminary unit economics.

---

## Slide 8: Go-to-Market

**[How you'll acquire users]**

- Primary channel: [One channel that's already working]
- Evidence: "We acquired our first [X] users through [channel] at $[X] CAC"
- Scale plan: "Next we'll expand to [channel 2] and [channel 3]"

One working channel > ten theoretical channels.

---

## Slide 9: Team

**[Why you're uniquely qualified]**

- **[Name]**, [Role] — [2-3 relevant sentences: domain expertise, previous company, unique insight]
- **[Name]**, [Role] — [2-3 relevant sentences]

Focus on why THIS team for THIS problem, not just credentials.

---

## Slide 10: The Ask

**Raising $[X]M on $[X]M post-money SAFE**

- Use of funds: [X]% product, [X]% hiring, [X]% growth
- What this round achieves: "[Milestone] in [X] months"
- Key hires: [1-2 critical roles]

---

## Design Notes
- Clean, minimal design. Content > decoration.
- Max 20 words per slide (except traction numbers)
- Dark text on light background (projector-friendly)
- Use screenshots, not mockups, wherever possible
- One idea per slide. If you have two ideas, make two slides.
