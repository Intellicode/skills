## Fundraise Timeline

### Pre-Batch Fundraising (Optional)
Some founders raise a pre-seed or SAFE round before or during early batch weeks:
- Use YC's post-money SAFE template
- Typical pre-seed: $500K-$2M on a $5M-$15M cap
- Only raise if you need runway to build during the batch
- Keep it simple, don't optimize for terms at this stage

### Batch Fundraising Timeline

| Week | Focus |
|------|-------|
| 1-4 | Build product, talk to users. Do NOT fundraise. |
| 5-6 | Start refining your narrative. Begin investor list. |
| 7-8 | Investor meetings begin (warm intros only). Don't pitch—share progress. |
| 9 | Demo Day prep. Finalize pitch. Practice 50+ times. |
| 10 | Alumni Demo Day (practice). Final investor outreach. |
| 11 | Demo Day |
| 12+ | Close round. Target: 2-4 weeks post Demo Day. |

## Pitch Deck

### YC-Style Pitch Deck Structure (10-12 slides max)

Use the template in [assets/pitch-deck-template.md](assets/pitch-deck-template.md) for a ready-to-use outline.

**Slide Order:**
1. **Company name + one-liner** — What you do in 5 words
2. **Problem** — The specific pain you solve. One concrete example.
3. **Solution** — How you solve it. Demo or screenshot preferred.
4. **Traction** — Revenue, users, growth rate. Numbers beat words.
5. **Market** — TAM/SAM/SOM or "if we're right, this market is huge"
6. **Product** — How it works. Short. Show, don't tell.
7. **Business model** — How you make money. Pricing. Unit economics if you have them.
8. **Go-to-market** — How you'll get users. One channel that's working.
9. **Team** — Why you're uniquely qualified. 2-3 relevant highlights per founder.
10. **Financials/Ask** — How much you're raising, on what terms, how you'll use it.

### Pitch Principles
- **Short sentences.** No paragraphs on slides. Bullet points ≤ 8 words.
- **Specific over vague.** "2,000 paying users" not "thousands of users."
- **Lead with strength.** If your traction is great, lead with that. If your team is the story, lead with that.
- **One idea per slide.** If you need two ideas, make two slides.
- **Decks are visual aids, not documents.** The story is you, not the slides.

### The 2-Minute Pitch (Demo Day Format)
- 150 words maximum for a 2-minute talk
- Write it out. Cut every word that isn't essential.
- Practice until you can deliver it without thinking about the words
- Eye contact > reading notes
- Energy > polish

### Common Pitch Mistakes
- Describing what you do instead of the problem you solve
- Saying "we're the X for Y" without explaining why that matters
- Vague traction ("growing fast" → how fast? what number?)
- Listing features instead of showing value
- Weak team slide ("we went to good schools" ≠ "we're uniquely qualified")

## SAFE Notes & Deal Terms

### Post-Money SAFE (YC Standard)
- Use YC's post-money SAFE template from ycombinator.com/documents
- Post-money SAFE = investor knows exactly what % they'll own
- Pre-money SAFE = ambiguous, can lead to tension with later investors
- YC strongly recommends post-money SAFE for simplicity

### Key Terms
| Term | What It Means | Typical Range |
|------|--------------|---------------|
| Valuation Cap | Max price per share investor pays | $4M-$20M for pre-seed/seed |
| Discount | % discount to next priced round | 0-20% (YC standard: none on post-money) |
| MFN | Most Favored Nation clause | Standard—lets investor match better terms |
| Pro Rata | Right to invest in future rounds | Usually included |

### Valuation Cap Guidance
- Too low → excessive dilution for founders
- Too high → signaling problems with next round
- YC companies typically raise on $8M-$20M caps (post-money) at Demo Day
- Cap should reflect: team quality + traction + market
- Don't over-optimize. Ship product, close round, get back to building.

### Cap Table Hygiene
- Use Pulley or Carta from day one
- Track every SAFE, option grant, and share issuance
- Keep it simple: founders + SAFEs + option pool
- No side letters with unusual terms
- No uncapped SAFEs unless you absolutely must

## Investor Outreach Strategy

### Building Your Investor List
1. **Tier 1 (4-6 investors):** Dream investors. Partners who know your space deeply.
2. **Tier 2 (8-12 investors):** Strong fit. Portfolio alignment. Good brand.
3. **Tier 3 (15-20 investors):** Reasonable options. Good enough if T1/T2 pass.

### Getting Warm Intros
- Ask YC partners for intros (they know who's active)
- Ask batchmates for intros to their investors
- Ask advisors/mentors
- Cold email works better than you think, especially with compelling traction
- Never send a cold LinkedIn message

### The Meeting Flow
1. **First meeting:** Tell your story. Show traction. Leave them wanting more.
2. **Follow-up:** Send updates. "Since we met, we've shipped X and grown Y%."
3. **Partner meeting:** If they're interested, you'll meet the full partnership.
4. **Term sheet → Close**

### Momentum Is Everything
- Run a tight process. Meet investors in parallel, not serially.
- Create FOMO naturally by sharing real progress updates
- Close with a deadline: "We're collecting commitments by [date]."
- First close is the hardest. After that, momentum builds.
- Don't wait for one investor. Move on faster than feels comfortable.

### Red Flags in Investors
- Wants unusual control provisions (board seats, veto rights)
- Slow to respond after expressing interest
- Asks for personal references from your previous cofounders
- Wants to renegotiate signed SAFEs
- Doesn't have fresh capital to deploy

## Demo Day Execution

### Pre-Demo Day Checklist
- [ ] Pitch written (150 words, 2 minutes)
- [ ] Pitch practiced 50+ times (time yourself, film yourself)
- [ ] Demo Day deck designed (if applicable—some Demo Days are talk-only)
- [ ] Investor list built (Tier 1/2/3 with warm intro paths)
- [ ] Key metrics updated in real-time dashboard
- [ ] Post-Demo Day follow-up email模板 prepared
- [ ] SAFE documents ready to send
- [ ] Cap table updated

### Demo Day Itself
- You'll deliver your pitch to 500+ investors
- Energy and clarity matter more than polish
- Don't read from a script. You should know your pitch cold.
- Specific numbers land better than adjectives
- The goal: investors remember you and reach out

### Immediately After Demo Day
1. Send follow-up emails within 1 hour to investors you met
2. Include: one-liner, traction numbers, link to demo/pitch deck, SAFE terms
3. Follow up every 2-3 days with progress updates
4. Close as fast as possible. The round is open until you say it's closed.

## Post-Demo Day Fundraise Management

### Tracking Your Round
- Keep a spreadsheet: Investor | Tier | Status | Last Contact | Next Step | Amount
- Update after every interaction
- Know your pipeline: how much is committed vs. interested vs. cold

### Typical Post-Demo Day Timeline
- **Week 1:** Heavy meeting volume. 5-8 meetings/day.
- **Week 2:** Second meetings, partner meetings, term sheets start coming.
- **Week 3-4:** Close commitments. Most YC rounds close within 2-4 weeks.

### Round Dynamics
- First check is the hardest. Once someone commits, others follow.
- You can set a close date to create urgency
- Don't optimize for the absolute best investor at the cost of speed
- Take money from people who will help you, not just anyone with a checkbook
- A $500K-$2M seed round is standard for YC companies post-Demo Day

### After the Raise
- Send a brief update to investors who passed (maintain the relationship)
- Set up monthly investor updates (use the template in assets/)
- Get back to building. The raise is not the milestone. The product is.
