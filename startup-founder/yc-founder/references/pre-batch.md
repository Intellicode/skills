## Timeline: Acceptance to Batch Start

Typically 6-10 weeks between acceptance and batch start. Use this time ruthlessly.

**Weeks 1-2: Foundation**
- Incorporate (Delaware C-Corp via Clerky or Stripe Atlas)
- Open startup bank account (Mercury, Brex, or Relay)
- Set up cap table (Carta or Pulley)
- Execute founder vesting agreements (standard: 4-year vest, 1-year cliff)
- Buy key domain names

**Weeks 3-4: Product Momentum**
- Ship an MVP. It does not need to be good. It needs to exist.
- Start talking to users immediately. 10 conversations minimum before batch starts.
- Set up basic analytics (Mixpanel, Amplitude, or PostHog free tier)
- Establish a weekly cadence: ship, measure, talk to users, repeat

**Weeks 5-6:batch Prep**
- Write a one-page brief on what you believe, what you're uncertain about, and what you want to get out of YC
- Prepare your 2-minute Demo Daypitch (you'll refine this later, but start practicing now)
- Read YC's "How to Start a Startup" lecture notes or watch the videos
- Connect with your batch mates in the YC intranet/forum

## Legal & Company Setup

### Incorporation Checklist
- [ ] Delaware C-Corp formation (use Clerky, Stripe Atlas, or experienced startup lawyer)
- [ ] Bylaws with standard startup provisions
- [ ] Authorize 10M shares of common stock (founders split ~8-9M, reserve ~1-2M for option pool)
- [ ] Founder restricted stock purchase agreements with 4-year vest, 1-year cliff
- [ ] Proprietary Information and Inventions Assignment Agreement (PIIA) from every founder
- [ ] Corporate resolutions authorizing initial actions
- [ ] Elect S-Corp tax status if eligible (talk to CPA, must file within 2.5 months of incorporation)
- [ ] Register as foreign corporation in your home state if operating from outside Delaware

### SAFE Notes
- [ ] Use YC's post-money SAFE template (available at ycombinator.com/documents)
- [ ] Choose valuation cap carefully—this sets the ceiling price for early investors
- [ ] Standard caps in 2024-2025 range: $4M-$10M for very early, $10M-$20M for traction
- [ ] Decide: Pro Rata side letter or not (YC standard is with pro rata)
- [ ] Keep a clean cap table. Use Pulley or Carta from day one.

### Banking & Financial
- [ ] Open business bank account (Mercury and Brex are popular with YC cos)
- [ ] Set up bookkeeping (Bench, Pilot, or QuickBooks)
- [ ] Establish a budget for the batch (aim for under $50K total burn for the 3 months)
- [ ] Set up corporate credit card
- [ ] File 83(b) elections within 30 days of stock purchase (critical tax election, non-revocable)

## Team Alignment

### Co-Founder Alignment Exercise
Before the batch, have an explicit conversation covering:

1. **Equity split** - Is it final? Are both founders comfortable? (Default: equal split unless reasons for asymmetry are agreed)
2. **Roles & decisions** - Who decides what? CEO/final decision-maker? How to resolve disagreements?
3. **Commitment level** - Full-time? Timeline expectations? What happens if one founder wants to leave?
4. **Vision alignment** - Where do you see this in 5 years? What's the exit vision? Are you aligned on "lifestyle business" vs "venture scale"?
5. **Personal financial situations** - Be honest about runway constraints and life commitments
6. **Non-compete/side projects** - Are either of you doing anything else?

### Single Founder Considerations
- YC strongly prefers co-founders, but accepts single founders
- You need to be exceptional at hiring early—plan your first 2-3 hires
- Consider finding a co-founder during pre-batch. YC's Co-Founder Matching can help.
- You will carry all operational load. Batch will intensify this.

## Idea Validation

### Pre-Batch Validation Checklist
- [ ] Can you state the problem in one sentence?
- [ ] Can you identify 10 specific people who have this problem?
- [ ] Have you talked to at least 10 potential users? (Not friends/family)
- [ ] Do users confirm the problem is painful? (Not just "nice to have")
- [ ] Can you articulate why existing solutions don't solve it?
- [ ] Is the market big enough? (For VC path: $1B+ TAM or clear path to it)
- [ ] Do you have a specific, testable hypothesis for the first version?

### Pivot Readiness
YC explicitly encourages pivots during the batch. Come in with strong convictions, loosely held:
- Write down your top 3 assumptions. If all 3 are wrong, what do you do?
- Pre-commit to reassessing after 2-3 weeks of talking to users
- Have 2-3 adjacent ideas ready in case the primary one doesn't work

## What to Prepare Before Arriving

### Mental Preparation
- The batch is intense. Expect to work harder than you ever have.
- You will get contradictory advice from different partners. That's the point— synthesize your own view.
- Rejection and criticism are data, not verdicts on you personally.
- Sleep and exercise are not optional. Burned-out founders make bad decisions.

### Practical Preparation
- [ ] Have a working product (even if ugly and barely functional)
- [ ] Set up basic deployment pipeline (Vercel, Railway, or whatever gets you shipping fastest)
- [ ] Have a list of 20+ potential users to talk to in the first week
- [ ] Clear your calendar of non-essential commitments for the next 3 months
- [ ] Set up a personal system for tracking tasks (Notion, Linear, or simple text file)
- [ ] Prepare a short "what we're working on" blurb for intros (2-3 sentences max)
