## Deck Structure Assessment

A strong pitch deck follows a logical narrative:

1. **Problem** - What painful problem exists? Who suffers?
2. **Solution** - How does this product solve it? Why now?
3. **Product** - What does it look like? Demo/screenshots.
4. **Traction** - What's actually working? Real metrics.
5. **Market** - How big is the opportunity? Bottom-up sizing.
6. **Business Model** - How do you make money? Unit economics.
7. **Competition** - Who else solves this? Why are you better?
8. **Team** - Why this team? Founder-market fit.
9. **Financials** - Key projections and assumptions.
10. **The Ask** - What do you need and what will it achieve?

Missing slides are a signal. If competition is omitted, founders may be naive. If traction is omitted, there may not be any.

## Slide-by-Slide Evaluation

### Problem Slide
**Strong:** Specific pain described from user perspective. Quantifiable cost of the problem.
**Weak:** Vague "X industry is broken" without specificity.

Evaluate:
- Is the problem articulated from the user's perspective?
- Is the cost of the problem quantified (time, money, frustration)?
- Is it a need-to-have or nice-to-have?
- How frequently does this problem occur?

### Solution Slide
**Strong:** Clear connection between problem and solution. "Why now" explained.
**Weak:** Generic technology-looking solution without user context.

Evaluate:
- Does the solution directly address the stated problem?
- Is there a compelling "why now" (tech shift, regulatory change, behavioral change)?
- Is the solution simple enough to explain in one sentence?

### Product Slide
**Strong:** Actual product screenshots/demo. Highlighting the "magic moment."
**Weak:** Mockups that look too polished. No evidence it works.

Evaluate:
- Does the product exist and work?
- Is there a "magic moment" (aha moment) that hooks users?
- Is the UX intuitive for the target user?

### Traction Slide
**Strong:** Revenue charts going up and to the right. Retention data. Growth rate.
**Weak:** "We have 10k signups" without engagement data.

The most important slide. Evaluate:
- Week-over-week or month-over-month growth rate
- Revenue (if applicable) and revenue growth
- Retention/cohort data
- Engagement metrics (DAU/MAU, sessions, time spent)
- Customer testimonials with specifics (not generic quotes)

### Market Slide
**Strong:** Bottom-up market sizing based on target customer count × price.
**Weak:** "The X market is $50B and we just need 1%."

Evaluate:
- Is the market sizing bottom-up (count customers × price)?
- Is the serviceable market realistic?
- Is the market growing or stagnant?
- What segments exist within the market?

### Business Model Slide
**Strong:** Clear pricing, unit economics, LTV/CAC data or path to it.
**Weak:** "We'll figure out monetization later."

Evaluate:
- Is the pricing model clear?
- Are there unit economics data (even early)?
- What's the path to positive LTV/CAC?
- Is there expansion revenue potential?

### Competition Slide
**Strong:** Honest landscape with clear differentiation axes.
**Weak:** No competitors listed, or 2x2 matrix where startup is top-right.

Evaluate:
- Are all real competitors acknowledged?
- Is differentiation honest and specific?
- Are incumbents' weaknesses accurate?
- Is there a clear competitive wedge?

### Team Slide
**Strong:** Relevant experience tied directly to the problem domain. Evidence of exceptional ability.
**Weak:** Impressive credentials unrelated to what they're building.

Evaluate:
- Is there founder-market fit? (Why THIS team?)
- Do they have relevant domain expertise?
- Can they build AND sell?
- What's the team composition gap?

### Financials Slide
**Strong:** Projections grounded in current traction rates. Assumptions stated clearly.
**Weak:** Hockey stick with no basis.

Evaluate:
- Are projections based on current traction rates?
- Are key assumptions stated?
- Is the path to profitability or next milestone clear?
- Do the numbers make sense (margins, growth rates)?

### The Ask Slide
**Strong:** Specific amount, clear use of funds, defined milestones.
**Weak:** "Raising $2M for growth" without specifics.

Evaluate:
- Is the raise amount reasonable for the stage?
- Is there a clear plan for the capital?
- What milestones will this funding achieve?
- Is there a runway plan (18-24 months)?

## Common Deck Failures

1. **No traction data** - Avoid investing until you see real usage
2. **Top-down market sizing** - "The market is huge" means nothing without bottom-up proof
3. **Ignoring competition** - "No one else does this" usually means no one wants it
4. **Confusing features with value** - Focus on outcomes, not capabilities
5. **Unrealistic projections** - 10x growth assumptions without basis
6. **No clear ask** - If you can't articulate what you need, you haven't thought it through
7. **Too long** - If you need 30 slides, you don't know your story yet
8. **Ego-driven team slide** - Credentials without context; what matters is why YOU for THIS problem

## Scoring Rubric

Rate each dimension 1-5, then compute overall signal:

| Dimension | Weight | Score (1-5) | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Problem severity | 2x | | |
| Solution clarity | 1x | | |
| Taction/momentum | 3x | | |
| Market opportunity | 2x | | |
| Business model viability | 1x | | |
| Competitive position | 1x | | |
| Team strength | 3x | | |
| Presentation quality | 1x | | |

**Strong pass:** Weighted score ≥ 35
**Conditional pass:** Weighted score 25-34 (needs follow-up)
**Pass:** Weighted score < 25
