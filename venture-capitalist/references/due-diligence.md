## Overview

Tailor diligence depth to the stage. Early-stage (Seed/Pre-seed) requires less formal diligence and more founder assessment. Later stages require thorough verification across all categories.

### Diligence Depth by Stage

| Category | Pre-seed | Seed | Series A | Series B+ |
|----------|----------|------|----------|-----------|
| Team | Deep | Deep | Moderate | Light |
| Product | Light | Moderate | Deep | Deep |
| Market | Moderate | Moderate | Deep | Deep |
| Financial | Light | Light | Moderate | Deep |
| Legal | Light | Moderate | Moderate | Deep |

## Team Diligence

### Founder Assessment
- [ ] Personal references (3+ per founder)
- [ ] Employment history verification
- [ ] Founder relationship history (how did they meet? how long have they worked together?)
- [ ] Founder-market fit: direct experience with the problem
- [ ] Technical ability to build the product
- [ ] Sales ability / communication skills
- [ ] Coachability: responsiveness to feedback during process
- [ ] Resilience: how they've handled setbacks
- [ ] Time commitment: full-time? other obligations?

### Team Dynamics
- [ ] Co-founder agreement and equity split (aim for near-equal, not 50/50 vesting)
- [ ] Decision-making process
- [ ] Complementary skills (builders + sellers)
- [ ] Key hires needed and recruitment plan

### Background Checks
- [ ] LinkedIn profile verification
- [ ] Academic credential verification (if claimed)
- [ ] Criminal background check (for key founders)
- [ ] Previous venture outcomes
- [ ] Conflicts of interest check

## Product Diligence

### Product Status
- [ ] Current product maturity (idea, MVP, production)
- [ ] Product development velocity (ship rate, iteration speed)
- [ ] Technical architecture assessment
- [ ] Technical debt overview
- [ ] IP/patents filed or planned
- [ ] Open source dependencies and license compliance

### User/ Customer Validation
- [ ] Number of active users/customers
- [ ] Engagement metrics (DAU/MAU, session frequency, time spent)
- [ ] Retention data (cohort analysis, 30/60/90-day retention)
- [ ] Net Promoter Score or equivalent
- [ ] Customer interviews (conduct 5-10 independent references)
- [ ] Customer acquisition channels and CAC
- [ ] Organic vs. paid acquisition mix

### Product-Market Fit Signals
- [ ] Retention curves flattening (not declining)
- [ ] Organic growth without marketing spend
- [ ] Users who'd be "very disappointed" without the product (>40%)
- [ ] Word-of-mouth referrals
- [ ] Increasing usage per user over time

## Market Diligence

### Market Sizing
- [ ] Bottom-up TAM/SAM/SOM calculation
- [ ] Market growth rate and trajectory
- [ ] Market timing factors (why now?)
- [ ] Adjacent markets for expansion

### Competitive Landscape
- [ ] Direct competitors identified and analyzed
- [ ] Indirect competitors and alternatives
- [ ] Competitive positioning analysis
- [ ] Defensive moats (network effects, data, switching costs)
- [ ] Market share estimates for key players

### Customer Discovery
- [ ] Total addressable customer count
- [ ] Customer willingness to pay (validated, not claimed)
- [ ] Sales cycle length
- [ ] Customer concentration risk

## Financial Diligence

### Current Financials
- [ ] Revenue (MRR/ARR if SaaS)
- [ ] Revenue growth rate (WoW, MoM)
- [ ] Gross margin
- [ ] Burn rate and runway
- [ ] Cash position
- [ ] Accounts receivable/payable

### Unit Economics
- [ ] Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- [ ] Lifetime Value (LTV)
- [ ] LTV:CAC ratio (target ≥ 3:1)
- [ ] Payback period
- [ ] Gross margin per customer
- [ ] Net revenue retention (for SaaS)

### Projections
- [ ] Revenue projections with assumptions
- [ ] Hiring plan and associated costs
- [ ] Path to profitability or next funding milestone
- [ ] Sensitivity analysis on key assumptions

## Legal Diligence

### Corporate
- [ ] Incorporation documents (Delaware C-Corp preferred)
- [ ] Cap table (clean, standard terms)
- [ ] Previous funding rounds and terms
- [ ] Board composition and governance
- [ ] Bylaws and shareholder agreements

### Intellectual Property
- [ ] Patent applications or grants
- [ ] Trademark registrations
- [ ] Trade secrets and confidentiality
- [ ] IP assignment from founders (ensure company owns IP)
- [ ] Open source license compliance

### Contracts & Obligations
- [ ] Customer contracts (key terms, renewal rates)
- [ ] Employee/contractor agreements (non-competes, IP assignment)
- [ ] Vendor contracts and commitments
- [ ] Lease obligations
- [ ] Outstanding litigation or threats

### Regulatory
- [ ] Industry-specific compliance requirements
- [ ] Data privacy compliance (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
- [ ] Required licenses or permits
- [ ] Material regulatory risks

## YC-Style Diligence Approach

YC's approach to diligence is founder-centric and fast. Key principles:

1. **Speed matters** - Don't drag the process out. If you can't decide in 2-3 conversations, the signal isn't there.
2. **Talk to users** - Spend more time talking to the startup's customers than analyzing spreadsheets.
3. **Watch what they do, not what they say** - Traction, GitHub commits, shipping velocity > pitch.
4. **Conviction over consensus** - A few deep convictions beat thorough checklists.
5. **Focus on the team** - For early-stage, the team IS the thesis. If you believe in the founders, most other things can be figured out.

### YC Diligence Priorities (in order)

1. **Can the founders build this?** (Technical + domain expertise)
2. **Do users want this?** (Real traction, not lip service)
3. **Is the market big enough?** (If first two are yes, market is usually bigger than it looks)
4. **Everything else** (Deal terms, legal, etc. are important but secondary)

### Red Lines (Immediate Pass)

- Founders who can't articulate the problem clearly
- No evidence of talking to users
- Dishonesty or misrepresentation during diligence
- No visible progress between meetings
- Cap table problems (dead founders, excessive advisor equity)
