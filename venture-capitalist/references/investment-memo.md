## Overview

An investment memo is the primary document for communicating an investment thesis to the investment committee or partners. It should be concise (2-4 pages), opinionated, and honest about risks.

The memo should help the reader answer one question: **"Should we invest?"**

## Memo Structure

A strong memo follows this structure:

1. **One-line thesis** — Why this investment makes sense in one sentence
2. **Company overview** — What they do, for whom, at what stage
3. **The bull case** — Why this could be a generational outcome
4. **The bear case** — What could go wrong and kill the investment
5. **Key risks and mitigants** — Identified risks with reasons they're manageable
6. **Recommendation** — Clear yes/no with conviction level

## Writing Guidelines

### Do
- **Be specific** — Use numbers, dates, and concrete examples over generalities
- **Show conviction** — State clearly whether you believe this is a good investment
- **Be honest about risks** — The best memos acknowledge what could go wrong
- **Write for skimmers** — Use headers, bullets, and bold for key points
- **Cite sources** — Attribute claims to specific conversations, data, or research
- **Include negative evidence** — What did you see that concerned you?

### Don't
- **Don't hedge excessively** — "Could potentially maybe become large" is useless
- **Don't bury the lead** — Put the thesis first, not last
- **Don't confuse market size with opportunity** — Big market ≠ big opportunity
- **Don't rely on top-down sizing** — "The market is $50B" means nothing
- **Don't write a love letter** — Balanced analysis beats cheerleading

## Examples of Strong Memos

### Example Thesis Statements

**Strong:**
> "We should invest in Ladder because their 15% WoW revenue growth, 92% net revenue retention, and founding team's decade of insurance experience positions them to become the default health insurance infrastructure for SMBs."

**Weak:**
> "Ladder is in the large healthcare market and has a good team."

### Example Bull Case Opening

**Strong:**
> "Ladder's product has achieved the rare combination of high growth AND high retention. They're growing 15% week over week with 92% net revenue retention — meaning customers not only stay, they expand. The founders (ex-Oscar Health, ex-Stripe) have intimate knowledge of both the insurance industry's pain points and modern infrastructure expectations. The timing is right: ACA enrollment grew 30% last year, and small businesses are being underserved by legacy carriers."

**Weak:**
> "Healthcare is a massive market and Ladder has strong founders with relevant experience."
